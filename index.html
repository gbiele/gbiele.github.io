Biography

Guido Biele is a research professor in the department for child health and development at the Norwegian Institute of Public Health. He has a background in experimental and cognitive psychology, and had later added functional magnetic resonance imaging, bayesian statics and (some) epidemiology to his researchers toolbox. His research interests include quantitative modeling of cognitive and neurobiological proccesses of decision making in Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) and in healthy populations as well as statistical modeling of risk factors for ADHD. He prefers when possible Bayesian estimation methods, and has recently become interested in the (im)possibilities of estimating causal effects from observational data.
